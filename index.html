Questions answered

Frequently asked questions

If you don’t see your question below, hit contact and we’ll respond in under 2 hours.

What is the difference between close testing and internal testing?

Close testing allows you to invite up to 100 testers via email for a pre-release track. Internal testing is limited to 100 testers but is designed for quick iterations; we help you properly configure close testing for Play Store requirements.

Can you publish using our Play Console account?

Yes. We can either guide you through publishing or publish on your behalf with a temporary console rental and strict access controls. We never store your credentials.

How long does the close testing cycle take?

Most apps are ready for review within 24-48 hours. The exact time depends on your app size and how quickly testers share feedback.

What do you include in the testing report?

Our report includes test coverage, crash logs, permission review, critical flows, and a checklist matching Play Console expectations for close testing submissions.

Do you support Flutter and native Android apps?

Yes. We support Flutter, Kotlin, Java, and cross-platform builds. We review your build artifacts (AAB/APK) and the associated Play Console configuration.
